Questions Answered

DISCLAIMER: We are not professionals and are unable to give you medical or psychological advice, just things we have experienced.

Anonymous asked:

It's my first day at a new school, what should I do? I don't know anyone! Please help!

TeenFAQ answered:

Hey! Ugh, don't we all hate that? It's the first day of highschool and you're trying your best to find a friend so that you won't have to sit all alone at lunch. We get it. Firstly, let me admit, I sat alone on the first day. I couldn't make a bff quick enough. Who cares! If you're alone on the first day, don't panic. It's perfectly normal, I swear. The key to success...is just being yourself. Be yourself, and you'll make friends. The worst thing that you can do is overthink it. If you're nerdy, be nerdy. If you're funny, be funny! There's nothing to it! It's as easy of 123. If you're yourself, people will be drawn to you. Don't force it, and definitely don't overthink it! -Xoxo TeenFAQ

Anonymous asked:

Hey TeenFAQ, I'm new to highschool and I'm not sure how to get scholarships. Any suggestions?

TeenFAQ answered:

Hey there, what's up! Yeah, we've all been there. Scholarships seem really tricky until you get the hang of it. But first, if you're a freshman or sophmore, don't worry too much about scholarships. Seriously! Just enjoy your school life. But if you're a junior or senior, you're right on track. Getting a scholarship is all about research, and capitalizing on your strengths. Just look up scholarships that match you and who you are! Basically like if you're a lefty, there's definitely a scholarship for that and etc. And dude, don't worry if your grades aren't amazing. Scholarships are seriously all about showing off your strengths. If your grades are okay, but your extracurriculars are off the charts, use it! No one's perfect, but everyone IS different. Make yourself standout and you'll get tons of great scholarships! -Xoxo TeenFAQ
