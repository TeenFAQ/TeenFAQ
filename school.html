Preparing For College

Let's talk testing.



It's a well-known fact that most tests are incredibly important, and that in highschool, you test ALOT. So, if you're wondering about TeenFAQ's most recommended testing straegies for any test. Continue reading!

So, let's get this straight. You're probably a highschool teenager that's geniunely worried about how to do better on some pretty major tests. That's your first mistake Stop worrying! Most people figure that if they don't pass a test or two, that their life will magically fall apart completely. It won't You need to stop stressing yourself out about a few questions. Seriously, chill out.

Second, I know that this step is pretty basic but it's essential: study. We all hate it, but we have to do it. You shouldn't let anything get in the way of you studying, not friends, not games, and definetly not sleep. You can sleep when you've graduated. Get atleast an hour of studying a day when you're trying to prepare for a test, however, never go above four hours of studying because then you're not really learning anymore. Pace yourself, and you'll do perfectly.

Hey! We have one more major step for you! Set realistic goals for yourself. I'm not saying that you should be a quitter, just be practical, and all-in-all completely and totally okay with your choice. For example, if you're scoring a 1000(new)/1500(old) on the SAT, don't aim to get a 2600 in a month. It's not realistic. Not because your incapable of it, but because you don't have enough time and because it's really hard to get that score. Aim high, but not too high. Know when to finish, but never ever quit. Remember, don't base your happiness on a score; you are worth so much more than that.

Clubs and Leadership Opportunities



There are many ways to get involved in your community during your High Schools years here are just some ideas:

• Be a part of Athletic teams, Student Government, Academic teams, or Art clubs.

• You can tutor your peers

• You can be part of the school newspaper or help with the year book.

• You can volunter in service opportunities

• Do after school activities



Creating a club:

• Choose something that you are passionate with as will be able to share your passion with others.

• Choose a goal that you want to accomplish through your club. It is important to choose short and long term goals to maximize the success of your club.

• Find a teacher or school staff to sponsor your club make sure that you are following the school's requirments when forming a club.

• Create fliers and spread the word around for students to join your club

• During your first club meetings plan events and activites for club memebers. Assign roles such as Presesident, Vice-President, Secretary, and Treasurer.

Some ideas for clubs are: service clubs, robotics club, a athletics club, book club, poetry club, tutoring club, dance club...

Jobs

Having a job during your teenage years could be a fun and useful way to earn money.


Choosing your prefect job:

* When applying for a job consider what you will be doing and if it is something that will enjoy.

* If possible choose a job that has something that you are passionate about for example if you like cooking consider being a chef or waiter. If you like interacting with poeple think about being a cashier, teacher assistant or a camp counsler. If you love animals consider being a dog walker or volunteer/ intern at animal shelter or vetenerian center.



Preparing for a job interview:

* Have a resume prepared and be ready to naswer questions during your interview

* Dress apropriate for the occasion

* Be confident during the interview and always think possitive. Do not forget to SMILE!!

* Have ideas of what you could bring to the job.

Job ideas: baby sitting, lifeguard, waiter, sales-person, pet sitting, cleaning serivice, tutoring...
